Florida is a diverse and dynamic state that attracts millions of visitors and residents with its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and economic opportunities. Florida has a population of about 22.2 million people, making it the third most populous state in the nation and the most populous in the Southeast. Florida's population is also very diverse, with more than one-fifth of its residents being foreign-born and more than one-fourth being Hispanic or Latino. Florida's population is also aging, with more than one-fifth of its residents being 65 years or older. ¹

Florida's economy is one of the largest and most varied in the country, with a gross state product of $1.4 trillion in 2022. Florida's economy is driven by several major sectors, including tourism, agriculture, aerospace, and arts and culture. Tourism is the largest industry in Florida, generating more than $67 billion in sales in 2017 and employing more than 1.3 million people. Florida is home to some of the world's most popular attractions, such as Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and the Kennedy Space Center. Agriculture is also a vital part of Florida's economy, producing more than $8 billion in cash receipts in 2017 and ranking first in the nation for citrus, sugarcane, tomatoes, and oranges. Aerospace is another key sector in Florida, contributing more than $19 billion to the state's economy in 2017 and supporting more than 130,000 jobs. Florida hosts several major aerospace companies and organizations, such as Boeing, Lockheed Martin, SpaceX, and NASA. Arts and culture are also important to Florida's economy, generating more than $4.6 billion in economic activity in 2017 and supporting more than 132,000 jobs. Florida has a rich and diverse cultural scene, featuring hundreds of museums, theaters, festivals, and galleries. ²³⁴

Florida's geography is also varied and distinctive, ranging from the subtropical wetlands of the Everglades to the sandy beaches of the Atlantic and Gulf coasts to the rolling hills of the Panhandle. Florida has a total area of about 65,800 square miles, making it the 22nd largest state by area and the second largest by water area. Florida has more than 8,400 miles of shoreline, the longest of any state except Alaska. Florida also has more than 30,000 lakes and ponds, covering about 3 million acres of land. Florida's climate is generally warm and humid, with mild winters and hot summers. However, Florida is also prone to hurricanes, tornadoes, floods, droughts, and wildfires that can cause significant damage and disruption. ¹⁵
